About

I work at the intersection of data science, evidence synthesis and health, combining rigorous systematic reviews and meta-analysis with predictive modelling to support clinical and health-policy decisions.

I am a Senior Data Scientist on the Evidence Synthesis team at IQVIA, where my work centres on meta-analysis, network meta-analysis and indirect treatment comparisons for health technology assessment.

I hold a PhD in Health Sciences from the Federal University of Pelotas (UFPel), with a research period at the University of Sao Paulo (USP), and an MBA in Data Science and Analytics from USP/ESALQ. I completed postdoctoral training in machine learning applied to mortality prediction. I have authored more than 100 peer-reviewed publications, most in international journals, and review for journals including The Lancet Regional Health Americas and the International Journal of Epidemiology.

Research focus

  • Evidence synthesis. Systematic reviews and meta-analysis of clinical and observational evidence, network meta-analysis, and anchored indirect treatment comparisons including population-adjusted methods (MAIC).
  • Machine learning for health outcomes. Predictive models for mortality, multimorbidity, cancer outcomes and health-service use, with attention to fairness across sex and race.
  • Health technology assessment. Post-incorporation monitoring and evidence reviews to support coverage and policy decisions, including work with Brazilian administrative health databases (DATASUS).
